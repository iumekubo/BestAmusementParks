Best Amusement Parks in Southern California

Amusement parks are fun places to go on a sunny day, but which one is the best to go to? Well, it really depends on what experience you want. Big thrills? Great photos? Find out what park is best for you!

Small Thrills

The Disneyland parks are great places for the whole family. They have great slow and rides for all ages. and some fast rides for young thrill seekers. The park is also very scenic, and you will get great photos.

Legoland is a fun park that is also good for the whole family. the entire park is Lego themed and is a work of art in itself. They also have a Lego-themed water park right next to the park.

Medium Thrills

Universal Studios is very fun for older kids, because some of their rides are a bit more intense. Most of the rides there are simulations, so they are a bit bumpy. The entire park is themed of shows that Universal owns, and it is very beautiful.

Big Thrills

Six Flags is the official thrill capital of the world, with 19 roller coasters (and one more on the way) they broke the record for most roller coasters in one park. They also have the first looping coaster ever made. They also have a water park right next to the park called Hurricane Harbor.

Knott's Berry Farm is a very fun park with lots of intense rides. One of the newest rides there is actually the first dive coaster in California. Soak City water park is right next to Knott's Berry Farm, so you can do both in one day.
